The Power of Flowers

Perfume is something that most kids enjoy smelling but aren't allowed to have. But everyone knows that perfume smells like flowers and pretty things, so let's try making some with items we can find outside. This recipe is wonderful to try on a beautiful summer day when the flowers are blooming and everyone wants to play outside. Making perfume is like a treasure hunt.

Ingredients

  • Bowl of Water
  • Rose Petals
  • Snowball Flowers
  • Daisies
  • Rosemary
  • Pine Needles
  • Additional Fragrant Items

Steps

  1. Place bowl of water outside and go through neighborhood to find next ingredients
  2. Gather rose petals from several different bushes. The bigger outer petals are best for smell and color
  3. Add the rose petals to the bowl
  4. Gather snowball flowers. If a large bunch is available, definitely pick it. Otherwise, small groupings will work fine
  5. Add the snowball flowers to the bowl
  6. Gather any other flowers that are in bloom and add to the bowl
  7. Pick some sprigs of rosemary from the neighbors herb garden
  8. Break off some pine needles from a large pine tree
  9. Add the rosemary and pine needles to the bowl
  10. Add any other additional fragrant items you find
  11. Stir the bowl's contents and allow to sit in the sun for one hour
  12. Pick out all of the flowers and other items from the water
  13. Smell the water. It probably won't smell like anything, so throw it away
